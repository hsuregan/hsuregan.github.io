I think that impressionism is my strength when it comes to art. From far away, an impressionist painting may be clearly of a building, or of a boat - the subject is often tangible - but on closer examination, you'll find a lot of 'imperfections' that collectively work together.

Sometimes I think, gee this is kinda ironic, since I'm also an engineer, and can be OCD about the smallest details. There's a playfulness about impressionism that encourages me to step back rather than hone in - to let the knots in my thought processes loose, and to think about the bigger picture. I find it cathartic.

I also have many, I would say, po-po-po-post modern, and 'non-formal' (I suppose), mixed media pieces. Drop me a line if you'd like to see an extended portfolio. (A lot of them are kind of outrageous.)

Definitely contact me if you'd like some work done. I'm open to most mediums and genres.
